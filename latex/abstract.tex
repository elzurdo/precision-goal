Sequential hypothesis testing offers flexibility over fixed-sample designs, but stopping
rules coupled to decision criteria risk confirmation bias through early peeking.
Methods that decouple the two, such as ``Precision is the Goal'' (PitG), eliminate this
bias by halting only once a target posterior precision $\omega_{\rm goal}$ is reached ---
yet PitG frequently yields inconclusive outcomes, most critically when the null hypothesis
is true.
We propose ``Decisive Precision is the Goal'' (DPitG), which requires \textit{both} the
precision target and a decisive hypothesis outcome to be satisfied simultaneously before
stopping.
A closed-form planning formula, $N_{\rm goal} \propto 1/\omega_{\rm goal}^2$, gives the
expected minimum sample size as a transparent lower bound before data collection begins.
In fair coin experiments with a minimum effect size of $\pm 0.05$, PitG's conclusiveness
ranges from $82.5\%$ to $0\%$ across precision goals $\omega_{\rm goal}=0.06$--$0.10$,
while DPitG's remains essentially flat at $97.2\%$--$97.9\%$ over the same span.
For instance, at $\omega_{\rm goal}=0.08$, DPitG reduces PitG's $63.3\%$ inconclusive
rate to just $2.3\%$, at a median sampling cost of only $4\%$ over $N_{\rm goal}$,
while preserving a zero false-positive rate.
Results are demonstrated on single-group binary data; the framework extends naturally
to continuous outcomes and two-group comparisons.
This advantage holds broadly across true and null parameter values, and DPitG's
fully pre-specified stopping rule is naturally compatible with pre-registration standards.
We provide an online interactive calculator and open-source code to support
prospective planning and interpretation of sequential experiments.
