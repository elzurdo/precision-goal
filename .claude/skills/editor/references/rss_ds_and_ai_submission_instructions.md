# RSS: Data Science and Artificial Intelligence - Submission Instructions

## Journal Overview

**RSS: Data Science and Artificial Intelligence** focuses on high-quality primary research papers targeting a broad audience spanning the entire breadth of data science (including AI, machine learning, statistics, econometrics, bioinformatics and more).

The journal is:
- **Peer-reviewed** with single-anonymized review (reviewers' identities hidden from authors)
- **Fully open access** publishing one continuous publication volume per year online
- Published by **Oxford University Press**
- **Online submission** via [ScholarOne Manuscripts](https://mc.manuscriptcentral.com/rssdat)

### Publication Timeline

- Accepted Manuscript published within one week on Advance Articles page
- Version of Record published after copyediting and proof review
- Automatic digital preservation via CLOCKSS, Global LOCKSS Network, and Portico

## Article Types

### Methods Paper—maximum word count: 6500

Methods Papers present an advance likely to make major impact in one or more applied fields. While theory (e.g. theorems concerning statistical or learning-theoretic properties) are welcome, this is not essential, but intuition and understanding of why the method works is important.

We are also very open to theory in a broader sense including examples and conjectures. Generally, we would expect empirical results to demonstrate effectiveness. However, in cases where the extent of methodological/conceptual innovation is large enough, results themselves may be illustrative and need not be of immediate high impact in any particular applied domain.

**Required sections:** Introduction, Methods, Results, Discussion, and Conclusion
(Discussion and Conclusion may be combined)

**Each submission must include:**

- Unstructured Abstract—maximum word count: 250
- Keywords—minimum 5, maximum 6
- Data availability statement
- References—no limit
- ORCID iD for all authors

**May include:**

- Tables and figures—no limit
- Graphical abstract (strongly encouraged)
- Back matter sections:
  - Acknowledgements
  - Author contributions (with CRediT details recommended)
  - Conflicts of interest
  - Funding

### Applications Paper—maximum word count: 6500

Applications Papers are expected to show clear potential for impact in one or more applied domains. Methods should be interesting and appropriate but need not be as novel as for methods papers. Work should be presented so as to be accessible to a broad data science audience, with appropriate explanation of domain-specific ideas.

At the same time, clear exposition of the core data scientific ideas involved in the method or application is important. Befitting the focus on data science per se, appropriate mathematical notation would normally be expected to be included in the main text.

**Required sections:** Introduction, Methods, Results, and Conclusion

**Each submission must include:**

- Unstructured Abstract—maximum word count: 250
- Keywords—minimum 5, maximum 6
- Data availability statement
- References—no limit
- ORCID iD for all authors

**May include:**

- Tables and figures—no limit
- Graphical abstract (strongly encouraged)
- Back matter sections:
  - Acknowledgements
  - Author contributions (with CRediT details recommended)
  - Conflicts of interest
  - Funding

### Behind the Scenes—maximum word count: 6500

Behind the Scenes papers present in detail data science approaches underpinning a major scientific result or practical application that is already published/available. Here, methodological novelty is less essential, as the focus is on communicating to fellow data scientists how a challenging problem has been addressed, including the conceptual link between the data science ideas and domain questions and the workflow itself.

For already published papers in very broad audience, pan-science journals with an important data science component, this route can complement the main paper by allowing detailed presentation of the data science itself. As for all RSS papers, relevant mathematical/technical exposition is welcome in the main text itself.

**Required sections:** Introduction, Methods, Results, and Conclusion

**Each submission must include:**

- Unstructured Abstract—maximum word count: 250
- Keywords—minimum 5, maximum 6
- Data availability statement
- References—no limit
- ORCID iD for all authors

**May include:**

- Tables and figures—no limit
- Graphical abstract (strongly encouraged)
- Back matter sections:
  - Acknowledgements
  - Author contributions
  - Conflicts of interest
  - Funding

## General Requirements

### Straightforward Formatting Requirements

We aim to make first submission as straightforward as possible and impose only minimal formatting requirements at this stage.

**For initial submission:**
- Any standard format is acceptable (e.g., article)
- Any conventional referencing style is acceptable
- LaTeX users may use [our LaTeX style file](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/oup-general-template/ybpypwncdxyb), but this is **not mandatory**

**Important:** Upon acceptance, papers must be reformatted using our style files.

### Writing Style and Use of Technical Terms

RSS: Data Science and Artificial Intelligence is aimed at a broad readership. However, unlike broad audience journals spanning all scientific fields, the focus is on the data sciences per se. At the same time, in contrast to venues focused on a particular field or subfield (AI, biostatistics, econometrics), results should be presented in such a way as to be understandable by readers from a wide range of (data science) fields.

#### Technical Material

RSS: Data Science and Artificial Intelligence is a data science journal and welcomes data scientific submissions with technical content (computational, mathematical, theoretical, conceptual…). While our audience is broad, most readers will be familiar with core, common concepts in data science, hence such notions can be assumed as background. That said, terms and ideas that are more specific to a specific field or subfield should be briefly explained.

**Standard background terms** (assume knowledge of these):
- Estimator
- Law of large numbers
- Cross-validation
- PCA
- Posterior density
- Graphical model

#### Use of Field-Specific Terminology

Terms and ideas that are not standard across the data sciences should be briefly explained on first appearance.

**Examples of field-specific terms requiring explanation:**
- Contrastive learning (AI)
- Inverse probability weighting (statistics)
- Skip connection (AI)
- Two-stage least squares (econometrics, statistics)
- Gene regulatory network (bioinformatics)
- Regression discontinuity design (econometrics, statistics)

For these terms, provide a brief explanation on first appearance that will help make your ideas accessible to readers from other fields. Example: "a gene regulatory network is a graph whose nodes are identified with genes and edges indicate biological or biochemical relationships between genes. Here we focus on networks with the following semantics…"

#### Main Text and Appendices

- Encourage presentation of data scientific material, including technical content, **in the main text**
- Open to various paper lengths
- **Main text should not greatly exceed 6500 words**
- May move some details to Appendices for clarity
- On first submission, we generally leave these choices to authors

#### Figures and Tables

- Overview/workflow type figures can be useful
- Relatively detailed figure captions are welcome, since they help readers understand key ideas in a self-contained fashion
- **No hard constraint on number** of figures or tables, only the general 6500-word main text constraint applies
- See detailed guidance below on figure accessibility and technical requirements

## Manuscript Preparation Instructions

Authors may wish to use a language-editing service before submitting to ensure that editors and reviewers understand the manuscript. Oxford University Press partners with **Enago**, a leading provider of author services.

### Title Page

The full title page should include:

- **Title** of the paper—avoid abbreviations
- **All full author names and affiliation addresses**
  - Include: Division/Department, Institution/Organization, City, and Country
- **Full mailing address and contact information of corresponding author**
  - Include: Division/Department, Institution/Organization, Full Street/Road Address, City, State abbreviation/Region, Postal Code/Zipcode, and Country
- **Abstract** (maximum 250 words)
- **Keywords** (minimum 5, maximum 6)
- **Introduction section** of main text

### Text Abstracts and Keywords

**Abstracts:**
- Written in English
- Unstructured format
- Maximum 250 words
- Should briefly outline the findings being presented
- **Must not** contain reference citations or abbreviations
- Should be included at beginning of main manuscript file and on title page
- Designed to summarise essential features in logical and concise sequence

**Keywords:**
- 5 or 6 keywords or key phrases
- Describe the content of the paper
- Helpful for choosing reviewers
- Include at the beginning of the main manuscript file

### Graphical Abstracts

Authors of all article types are **strongly encouraged** to submit a graphical abstract in addition to the text abstract.

- Should convey the key message to editors and general readership
- Should enhance the article's impact
- Will be published online as part of table of contents and in PDF
- Submit as a **separate file** for peer review
- File naming example: `graphical_abstract.tiff`
- Use appropriate file format and resolution (see figure guidance below)

### Acknowledgements

Authors of all article types are encouraged to submit an "Acknowledgements" back matter section.

- Set as a **single paragraph** with separate heading
- Include at **end of manuscript**
- Author names should be given as **closed initials with dots** (e.g., C.J.D.)
- **Do not** include study funding or conflict of interest information here (use Funding or Conflicts of Interest sections instead)

**Order for listing within Acknowledgements:**
1. Thanks to colleagues who assisted in the study
2. Special contribution circumstances (e.g., "The authors contributed equally to the study.")
3. Thanks for personal assistance (e.g., manuscript preparation)

### Funding

Authors of all article types are encouraged to submit a "Funding" back matter section.

- Clearly labeled with separate heading
- Include at end of manuscript
- **Must fully declare** all funding information relevant to the study, including **specific grant numbers**
- Use **Crossref funder registry** names exactly if funder is listed there
- Identify author recipients of grants using **closed initials with dots** (e.g., C.J.D.)
- If no funding: state "None declared."

### Style

Please refer to [journal style requirements](https://static.primary.prod.gcms.the-infra.com/static/site/rssdat/document/RSS_Mini_Style_Checklist.docx) when preparing your manuscript.

**Key style points:**
- **UK spelling** throughout (except in quotations and references)
- Nonstandard **abbreviations must be defined** at first occurrence
- Abbreviations in **capital letters without full stops**

#### Lists of Displayed Items

Text lists of items should be identified as sequences: (a), (b), (c), ...

Further itemization within these: (i), (ii), (iii), ...

**Not used:** 'Bullet' points, (A), (B), (C), ..., (I), (II), (III), ... or other symbols

### Theorems, Propositions, and Corollaries

- Number **sequentially and individually by type** (not by section)
- Examples: theorem 1, theorem 2, proposition 1, proposition 2 ...
- **Not:** theorem 1, proposition 2, theorem 3, proposition 4...
- **Not:** theorem 2.1, theorem 3.1, ...

### Mathematical Equations

- Complicated expressions should be **typed on separate lines** ('displayed')
- Important equations should be **numbered consecutively**
- Should be **centred** along the line and **numbered to the right**
- **Numbering format:** (1), (2), (3), ... (preferred) or (2.1), (2.2), (2.3), ...
- **For groups:** (1a), (1b), (1c), ... is acceptable; (1'), (1''), ... is **not**
- **Matrices** indicated by italics
- **Vectors** indicated by bold
- Development of mathematical expressions, if essential, should be in **appendices**
- Only relevant equations in **body of text**
- Each **appendix** should have a short title

#### Additional Mathematical Style Rules

- **Brackets:** order in nested expressions is [{()}]
- **Exponential function:** use 'exp' rather than 'e' in text and with complicated arguments

### Tables

Authors **must number all tables** (e.g., table 1, table 2, table 3) and **reference them within the text in chronological order**.

**Table Format:**
- Supply in **editable format** (e.g., Microsoft Word)
- **Not embedded as image file**
- Can be included within text near point of mention **or** separately at end of manuscript

**Table Content:**
- Should **not duplicate** information already given in text
- Should **not contain** material better presented graphically
- **Avoid excessive formatting** (colour, shading not replicated in published web version)
- Avoid tabbed spacing to indicate alignment
- **Tabular matter should be simple** with brief column headings and minimum columns
- **No vertical lines** separating columns
- **No horizontal rules** within body of table
- Numbers **right justified** or with decimal points lined up
- Numbers **appropriately rounded**

**Table Features:**
- Provide **units in column or row headers**, not in table body
- Ensure any **formatting or superscript symbols** (asterisks) explained in table footnote
- **Order of symbols for footnotes:** †, ‡, §, §§, *, **, ††, ‡‡

### Figures

#### Acceptable File Formats

Figures must be submitted in one of these formats:
- .jpeg
- .jpg
- .png
- .tiff
- .pdf
- .eps

**Not accepted:** .bmp, .gif, .doc/.docx

#### Figure Captions and Identification

- Include **figure titles, legends, and captions within the manuscript file**
- **Not included in image files**
- **Remove keys from figures** — put material into caption instead
- If accepted for publication, provide **short captions in list** on separate page at end of manuscript (double line spacing)

#### File Naming and Citation

- File names should be **simple and match citation** (e.g., fig1.tiff, fig2.eps)
- Cite **consecutively within text**
- Submit **each figure as single individual image file**
- Multi-panel figures: **all panels as one single figure file**
- Each panel should be **labelled with single capital letter** (A, B, C, D – no brackets or periods) in **upper-left corner**

#### Ensure Permissions

Authors must have permission to re-use or adapt any third-party image materials.

#### Image Types and Formats

**Vector Graphics** (maps, charts, graphs, shapes, diagrams):
- Best rendered as geometric forms
- Use mathematical relationships between points and lines to describe image
- Resolution does not apply
- Save as **.eps or .pdf files**
- **Embed the fonts**
- All graphs should have brief, clear, **self-explanatory title** with unit information
- **Carefully choose scales** and **clearly label both axes**

**Raster Images** (photographs, paintings, scans):
- Save as **uncompressed .tiff files** to avoid quality loss
- .jpg/.png acceptable but may result in lower resolution
- Resolution measured in dots or pixels per area (dpi or ppi)

**Minimum Resolution Requirements:**
- Colour half-tones: **350 dpi**
- Grayscale half-tones: **600 dpi**
- Combination half-tones and line art: **600-900 dpi**
- Monochrome line art (complex or finely drawn): **1200 dpi**

#### Figure Accessibility and Alt Text

**Accessibility Requirement:** All figures must include alt text (alternative text) descriptions.

**Guidelines:**
- Consider accessibility when designing figures
- Ensure all images easily understood by colour-blind and visually-impaired readers
- Refer to [OUP detailed guidance on figure accessibility](https://static.primary.prod.gcms.the-infra.com/static/umbrella/document/Figures_accessibility_journals_edition_v2.2_2024.pdf), including recommendations for:
  - Colour palettes
  - Colour contrast
  - Image layout
  - Text accessibility

**Alt Text Submission:**
- Include each alt text description in **main manuscript file**
- Place **directly under figure legend**
- Precede with **'Alt text:'**
- Follow [detailed guidance on drafting and submitting alt text](https://static.primary.prod.gcms.the-infra.com/static/site/journals/document/alt-text-figure-accessibility.pdf)

**Benefits:**
- Accessible to screen reader users
- Supports visually impaired readers
- Alt text is only accessible via e-reader, not in typeset article
- Demonstrates commitment to accessibility
- Enhances overall impact and reach of work

## Peer Review Process

### Review Type

The Journal operates **single-anonymized peer review:**
- Authors' identity known to editors and reviewers
- Reviewers' identities hidden from authors and known only to editors

### Review Process

1. Submitted manuscript passes **initial assessment by Editor-in-Chief**
2. Passed to **handling editor** for peer review oversight
3. Handling editor recommends final decision
4. **Editor-in-Chief makes final decision** on manuscript

### Conflict of Interest

- Editors and reviewers must not handle manuscripts if they have conflict of interest with authors or content
- Editors make every effort to avoid potential conflicts in assigning reviewers
- Recommended reviewers must be able to provide objective assessment without financial or interpersonal conflicts

### Suggesting Reviewers

- Authors **may suggest potential reviewers** at submission
- **No guarantee** suggested reviewers will be selected
- Recommended reviewers should be:
  - Experts in their field
  - Able to provide objective assessment
  - From diverse range of backgrounds, including under-represented communities

### Manuscript Transfer

#### Receiving Transfers
Journal accepts transfers from other Royal Statistical Society journals. Reviewer reports may be included with reviewer consent. Transferred manuscripts may be sent for additional peer review.

#### Sending Transfers
Editorial team may offer option to transfer manuscript to another Royal Statistical Society journal if not suitable. Manuscript files and consenting reviewer reports will be sent to receiving journal.

### Screening for Misconduct

Manuscripts may be screened using:
- **iThenticate** to detect publication misconduct (plagiarism, redundant publication)
- **Third-party services** to detect integrity issues (inappropriate image alteration, papermill activity)

### Appeals and Complaints

**Appeals:**
- Authors may appeal editorial decision
- Contact editorial office at [journal@rss.org.uk](mailto:journal@rss.org.uk)
- Provide specific details about why decision should be reconsidered
- **Do not resubmit** manuscript while appeal pending
- Expect response within reasonable timeframe

**Complaints:**
- Complaints regarding non-editorial decisions, policies, procedures, editors, or staff
- Carried forward following COPE guidelines
- Appropriate sanctions enacted if deemed necessary

## Publication and Research Ethics

### Authorship

Authorship is limited to those who have made a **significant contribution to the design and execution** of the work described.

**Important Notes:**
- Journal will contact **all listed authors** at submission to confirm their role
- Detailed definition from [ICMJE definitions of authors and contributors](https://www.icmje.org/recommendations/browse/roles-and-responsibilities/defining-the-role-of-authors-and-contributors.html)
- Journal **does not allow:**
  - **Ghost authorship** (unnamed author prepares article with no credit)
  - **Guest/gift authorship** (author made little or no contribution)

#### AI Use Disclosure

- **Natural language processing tools driven by AI do not qualify as authors**
- Journal will screen for them in author lists
- **AI use disclosure required** in cover letter to editors and in Methods or Acknowledgements section of manuscript
- See [COPE position statement on Authorship and AI](https://publicationethics.org/cope-position-statements/ai-author)

#### Authorship Changes

- **No authorship changes** (list, order, or corresponding author designation) without editor approval after submission
- **All co-authors must agree**
- Journal does not mediate disputes
- If individuals cannot agree: [contact editorial office](mailto:journal@rss.org.uk)
- Dispute must be resolved before publication

#### ORCID iD

**Requirement:** Submitting authors must provide [ORCID iD (Open Researcher and Contributor ID)](https://orcid.org/) at submission.

- If you don't have an ORCID iD: register for free at [ORCID website](https://orcid.org/)
- ORCID identifiers included in papers and displayed online (HTML and PDF versions)
- Complies with ORCID recommended practice
- Authors can link ORCID iD to submission account for future submissions

### Disclosure of Potential Conflicts of Interest

**All authors required** to disclose potential conflicts of interest at submission point.

**Responsibility:** Corresponding author ensures **all author conflicts declared** to journal.

**Conflicts inclusion:** Should be included in cover letter to Editor.

**Definition:** A conflict of interest exists when position, activities, or relationships (direct/indirect, financial/non-financial) could influence or be seen to influence individual's opinions or activities.

**At Editor's discretion:** Information may be printed at end of published paper

**If no conflicts:** State "none declared"

**Guidance:**
- [OUP's definition of conflict of interest](https://academic.oup.com/pages/authoring/journals/preparing_your_manuscript/ethics#conflict)
- [COPE guidance for undisclosed conflicts](https://doi.org/10.24318/cope.2019.2.6)

- New theoretical findings

**Insufficient changes/additions:**
- Stylistic rewrites or formatting changes
- Inclusion of proofs left out of original
- Minor extensions to experiments

**Decision:** Editorial team and reviewers determine if changes substantial enough

### Research Misconduct

Authors should observe high standards per [COPE guidelines](http://publicationethics.org/resources/guidelines).

**Unacceptable practices:**
- Falsification or fabrication of data
- Inappropriate image manipulation
- Plagiarism
- Duplicate publication without proper citation
- Misappropriation of work

**Investigations:**
- Allegations treated seriously
- Investigated per [COPE guidance](https://publicationethics.org/guidance/Flowcharts)
- Both direct and social media allegations considered

#### Consequences of Established Misconduct

If misconduct established beyond reasonable doubt:

- **Under consideration:** Manuscript rejected and returned
- **Already published:** Depending on nature/severity, either:
  - Correction notice published and linked to paper
  - Retraction following [COPE Retraction Guidelines](https://publicationethics.org/retraction-guidelines)
- **Institution(s) and other journals:** May be informed

#### Screening and Definitions

Manuscripts may be screened with **plagiarism-detection software**, especially if reason to suppose prior publication.

**COPE Definitions:**

- **Plagiarism:** "When somebody presents the work of others (data, words or theories) as if they were their own and without proper acknowledgment"

- **Redundant/Overlapping Publication:** "When a published work (or substantial sections) is published more than once without adequate acknowledgment, or when same/substantially overlapping data presented in multiple publications without adequate cross-referencing, particularly when unlikely that reviewers/readers realize findings published before"

- **Citation Manipulation:** "Behaviours intended to inflate citation counts, such as: excessive self-citation, excessive journal self-citation, excessive coordinated citation between journals"

- **Data Fabrication:** Intentionally creating fake data or misrepresenting results (e.g., making up datasets)

- **Data Falsification:** Manipulating research data to give false representation (cropping gels/images, omitting data, etc.)

**Guidelines:** [C4DISC Guidelines on inclusive language and images](https://c4disc.pubpub.org/pub/v4up8c2n/release/1?readingCollection=8dea2bef)

### Accessibility

Written, visual, and audio content must be accessible to all.

**Guidance:** [C4DISC guidelines](https://c4disc.pubpub.org/pub/v4up8c2n/release/1?readingCollection=8dea2bef) for making text, images, charts, tables, audio and video accessible.

### Data and Materials Availability

**Requirement:** Authors must **publicly release all data and software code** underlying any published paper (where ethically possible) as condition of publication.

**Data Availability Statement:**
- **Required** in paper
- Separate section before Acknowledgements
- Should describe and provide means of access
- Link to data or provide unique identifier where possible

**Minimum information:** Data and software citations should include persistent identifiers/DOIs

**References:** [OUP guidance on citing research data and software](https://academic.oup.com/pages/open-research/research-data#Citing%20research%20data%20and%20software)

**Data presentation:**
- Whenever possible, present in main manuscript or supporting files
- **Or** deposit in public repository

**Repository guidance:** [OUP's Research data page](https://academic.oup.com/pages/open-research/research-data#Choosing%20where%20to%20archive%20your%20data) for selecting repositories by subject area

### Choosing Where to Archive Your Data

Authors highly encouraged to select **repository that issues DOIs** for persistent linking.

**Exploration tools:**
- [FAIRsharing.org](https://fairsharing.org/)
- [re3data.org](https://www.re3data.org/)

**Recommended repositories:**
- [Dryad Digital Repository](https://datadryad.org/stash)
- [Figshare](https://figshare.com/)
- [GitHub](https://github.com/)

**Considerations:**
- Certification status
- Services offered
- Discipline-specific repositories available

### Digital Preservation

Content published in Journal automatically deposited into digital preservation services:
- **CLOCKSS**
- **Global LOCKSS Network**
- **Portico**

**Purpose:** Ensures long-term preservation. Through LOCKSS, participating institutions can sustain access if Journal otherwise unavailable.

**Access triggers:** If Journal ceases publishing or content becomes permanently unavailable, long-term access through CLOCKSS and Portico archives triggered.

**Note:** Until trigger event, content not available publicly through these services.

### Self-Archiving Policy

Self-archiving: posting copy on publicly accessible website or repository.

**Permitted under certain circumstances:**
- Author's own webpage
- Institutional webpage
- Other repositories

**Details:** [Author self-archiving policy](https://academic.oup.com/journals/pages/self_archiving_policy_c)

### Publishing Agreements

After acceptance, authors asked to sign licence to publish through **SciPris** (licencing and payment portal).

**Corresponding author responsibility:** Arrange payment of open access charge

**Benefit of OA charge:** Published papers immediately and freely available to all readers upon online publication

**Note:** Editorial decisions occur prior to payment step and not influenced by payment or ability to pay

#### Licence Types Available

- **Creative Commons Attribution licence (CC BY)**

### Charges

#### Open Access Charges

As fully open access journal, **must pay open access charge** or request use of institutional agreement through SciPris.

**Charge varies by manuscript category** (see journal website for current rates)

#### Low and Middle Income Countries

Corresponding authors based in LMIC countries can receive **discount** or **waiver**.

**Details:** [APC waiver and discount policy](https://academic.oup.com/pages/open-research/open-access/charges-licences-and-self-archiving/apc-waiver-policy)

**Participating authors:** Institution may pay charge

**Eligibility:** Corresponding author must provide qualifying institution as **primary affiliation** at submission

**Important:** After submission, changing corresponding author to access Read and Publish funding **not permissible**

**Find participating institutions:** [OUP's list of participating institutions](https://academic.oup.com/pages/open-research/read-and-publish-agreements/participating-journals-and-institutions)

#### Other Charges

- **Colour:** Journal does not charge for colour
- **Page charges:** Journal does not have page charges

#### Member Discounts

Corresponding authors who are **Royal Statistical Society members** may be eligible for **discounts** on publication charges.

**Provide member identifier** when prompted at submission

**Discount available:** 20% member discount for CC BY OA

#### Funder Mandates

Some funders require open access publication as funding condition.

**Author responsibility:** Clarify any OA requirements with funder or institution before selecting licence

**Guidance:** [OUP funder mandates page](https://academic.oup.com/pages/open-research/open-access/complying-with-funder-policies) with links to funder policies
